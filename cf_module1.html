1. The Goal of Corporate Finance Ch. 1

Corporate Finance answers three fundamental questions: What long-term investments should the firm make? (Capital Budgeting), How should the firm raise funds? (Capital Structure), and How should short-term cash flows be managed? (Working Capital Management).

🏭

Capital Budgeting

Which long-term projects to invest in? Tata Steel deciding whether to build a new plant.

💰

Capital Structure

Equity vs. debt mix. Reliance Industries choosing how to fund a ₹75,000 cr investment.

🔄

Working Capital

Managing day-to-day cash, receivables and payables. Infosys optimising its cash cycle.

Core Principle: The goal of the firm is to maximise shareholder wealth — i.e. maximise the current stock price. This differs from maximising profit (which ignores risk and timing).

2. Time Value of Money Ch. 4

A rupee today is worth more than a rupee tomorrow — because today's rupee can be invested to earn a return. This single idea underpins almost every financial decision.

Future Value
FV = PV × (1 + r)t
PV = Present Value  |  r = interest rate per period  |  t = number of periods
Present Value
PV = FV / (1 + r)t  =  FV × DF
DF = Discount Factor = 1/(1+r)t
Indian Market Example

You deposit ₹1,00,000 in an SBI FD at 7% p.a. for 3 years.

FV = 1,00,000 × (1.07)³ = ₹1,22,504

Reverse: what is the PV of ₹1,22,504 receivable in 3 years at 7%?

PV = 1,22,504 / (1.07)³ = ₹1,00,000 ✓

3. Annuity & Growing Annuity Ch. 4

An annuity is a series of equal cash flows at regular intervals. A growing annuity has cash flows that grow at a constant rate g each period.

PV of Annuity (ordinary — first CF at t=1)
PV = C × [ 1 – 1/(1+r)t ] / r
C = cash flow per period
PV of Growing Annuity
PV = C / (r – g) × [ 1 – ((1+g)/(1+r))t ]
g = growth rate per period  (r ≠ g)
Home Loan EMI — HDFC Bank

Loan: ₹50,00,000 at 8.5% p.a. (i.e. 0.708% per month) for 20 years (240 months).

EMI = PV × r / [1 – (1+r)–n] = 50,00,000 × 0.00708 / [1 – (1.00708)–240] ≈ ₹43,391/month

4. Perpetuity & Growing Perpetuity Ch. 4

♾️

Perpetuity

Equal CF forever.
PV = C / r

📈

Growing Perpetuity

CF grows at g% forever.
PV = C / (r – g)

Example — Preference Share Valuation

Tata Steel preference shares pay a ₹12 annual dividend forever. If required return = 10%, intrinsic value = 12/0.10 = ₹120 per share.

If dividends grow at 3% p.a., value = 12/(0.10–0.03) = ₹171.43 — demonstrating why growth matters enormously.

5. Nominal vs Effective Rate of Return Ch. 4

Banks quote a nominal (stated) rate, but compounding frequency determines what you actually earn — the Effective Annual Rate (EAR).

Effective Annual Rate
EAR = (1 + r_nom/m)m – 1
m = number of compounding periods per year
Nominal RateCompoundingEARIndian Example
8.0%Annual8.00%Some Govt bonds
8.0%Quarterly8.24%LIC endowment plans

8.0%Daily8.33%Credit card revolving balance
💡

RBI Regulation: SEBI requires mutual funds to advertise returns as annualised (EAR-equivalent). Credit card companies must disclose the annualised percentage rate — which can be 36–42% p.a. even if they quote 3% per month!
