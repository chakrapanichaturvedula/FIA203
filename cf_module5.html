1. What is Cost of Capital? Ch. 13

The cost of capital is the minimum required return a firm must earn on its investments to satisfy its financiers — equity holders and debt holders. It is used as the discount rate (hurdle rate) in NPV analysis.

📘

Cost of Equity (Kₑ)

Return required by shareholders. Higher risk → higher Kₑ. Estimated via CAPM or Gordon Growth Model.

🏦

Cost of Debt (Kd)

Return required by lenders. Use YTM of existing bonds or loan interest rate — adjusted for tax shield.

⚖️

WACC

Blended cost using market-value weights of equity and debt. The correct hurdle rate for firm-wide decisions.

2. Cost of Equity (Kₑ) Ch. 13

Two approaches to estimate Kₑ:

Method 1 — CAPM
Kₑ = Rƒ + β × (Rₘ – Rƒ)
Most commonly used. Requires risk-free rate, beta, and market risk premium.
Method 2 — Gordon Growth (Dividend Discount)
Kₑ = D₁ / P₀ + g
D₁ = next dividend  |  P₀ = current market price  |  g = sustainable dividend growth rate
HDFC Bank — Kₑ Estimation

CAPM: Rƒ = 7%, β = 0.85, Market Risk Premium = 6%

Kₑ = 7% + 0.85×6% = 7% + 5.1% = 12.1%

DDM check: P₀ = ₹1,650, D₁ = ₹19, g = 10%

Kₑ = 19/1650 + 0.10 = 1.15% + 10% = 11.15% (reasonably close — use CAPM as primary)

3. Cost of Debt (Kd) & Tax Shield Ch. 13

Interest payments on debt are tax-deductible in India. The government effectively subsidises debt financing by the corporate tax rate. The after-tax cost of debt reflects this saving.

After-Tax Cost of Debt
Kd (after-tax) = YTM × (1 – T)
T = corporate tax rate (India: typically 25.17% for new domestic cos. under Section 115BAA)
Tata Steel Bond

Tata Steel issues a bond with YTM = 9%. Corporate tax rate = 25%.

After-tax Kd = 9% × (1 – 0.25) = 9% × 0.75 = 6.75%

The government bears 25% of the interest cost via tax savings — making debt cheaper than equity.

💡

Modigliani–Miller Tax Shield: The value of the tax shield on debt = T × D (tax rate × debt outstanding). This is why most Indian companies use some debt in their capital structure — it creates value through the tax shield.

4. WACC — The Weighted Average Ch. 13

WACC Formula
WACC = wₑ×Kₑ + wd×Kd×(1–T)
wₑ = E/(E+D) = equity weight  |  wd = D/(E+D) = debt weight  |  Use MARKET values, not book values
Larsen & Toubro — WACC Calculation

Market cap (E) = ₹3,00,000 cr. Total debt (D) = ₹1,00,000 cr. Total capital = ₹4,00,000 cr.

wₑ = 3,00,000/4,00,000 = 75%  |  wd = 1,00,000/4,00,000 = 25%

Kₑ (CAPM) = 13%. Pre-tax Kd = 8%. Tax rate = 25%.

WACC = 0.75×13% + 0.25×8%×(1–0.25) = 9.75% + 1.50% = 11.25%
