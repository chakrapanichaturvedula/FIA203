1. Overview of Capital Budgeting Ch. 5

Capital budgeting is the process of evaluating and selecting long-term investments that are consistent with the firm's goal of maximising shareholder wealth. These are major decisions — factory expansions, new product lines, acquisitions — that commit large sums for many years.

✅

NPV

Net Present Value — theoretically superior. Accept if NPV > 0.

ACCEPT: NPV > 0
📉

IRR

Internal Rate of Return. Accept if IRR > Cost of Capital.

ACCEPT: IRR > r
📆

Payback Period

Years to recover initial investment. Simpler but ignores TVM.

REJECT: PP > Cutoff
📊

ARR

Accounting Rate of Return = Avg Net Income / Avg Book Value.

ACCEPT: ARR > Target

2. Net Present Value (NPV) Ch. 5

Net Present Value
NPV = –C₀ + CF₁/(1+r) + CF₂/(1+r)² + … + CFₙ/(1+r)ⁿ
C₀ = initial investment  |  CF = cash flow  |  r = discount rate (cost of capital)

NPV Rule: Accept a project if NPV > 0 (it creates value). Reject if NPV < 0. Among mutually exclusive projects, choose the one with the highest positive NPV.

Tata Motors — New EV Plant

Initial investment = ₹500 crore. Expected cash flows over 5 years: ₹80, ₹120, ₹150, ₹160, ₹200 crore. Cost of capital = 12%.

NPV = –500 + 80/1.12 + 120/1.12² + 150/1.12³ + 160/1.12⁴ + 200/1.12⁵

NPV = –500 + 71.43 + 95.66 + 106.76 + 101.68 + 113.48 = –₹10.99 crore

Decision: Reject — the project destroys value at 12% cost of capital.

3. Internal Rate of Return (IRR) Ch. 5

The IRR is the discount rate that makes NPV = 0. It is found by trial and error (or interpolation). Accept if IRR exceeds the required rate of return.

IRR Definition
0 = –C₀ + Σ CFₜ/(1+IRR)t
Solve for IRR — the rate that makes NPV = 0
⚠️

IRR Pitfalls: (1) Multiple IRRs when cash flows change sign more than once. (2) IRR ignores the scale of investment — a small project with 30% IRR may be worse than a large one at 15%. (3) For mutually exclusive projects, always use NPV as the final arbiter.

5. Incremental Project Cash Flows Ch. 6

When evaluating a project, only incremental (relevant) cash flows matter — flows that occur because of the decision. Sunk costs are irrelevant; opportunity costs and side effects (cannibalisation) must be included.

🚫

Sunk Costs

Already spent — irrelevant. A ₹50 cr feasibility study already done should NOT be included.

🔄

Opportunity Costs

What you give up. Using own land has an opportunity cost = its market rental value.

💥

Side Effects

Cannibalisation — new Maruti CNG car reducing sales of existing petrol model.

🏭

Working Capital

New project needs inventory/receivables — cash outflow at start, recovered at end.

Project Cash Flow Structure

Year 0: –Equipment cost – Working Capital investment

Year 1–n: (Revenue – Costs – Depreciation) × (1 – tax) + Depreciation = NOPAT + D&A

Final Year (extra): + Salvage value (after tax) + Recovery of Working Capital
